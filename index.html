Dan Kolov's life in short

  • Dan Kolov was a Bulgarian wrestler born on December 24, 1892, in Plovdiv, Bulgaria.
  • He began wrestling at a young age and quickly rose to become one of Bulgaria's most successful wrestlers.
  • Kolov won numerous national and international wrestling championships throughout his career, including three Olympic medals.
  • He also served in the Bulgarian army during World War I and was later decorated for his bravery.
  • After retiring from wrestling, Kolov became a wrestling coach and trained several successful wrestlers in Bulgaria and abroad.
  • He passed away on May 18, 1940, at the age of 47, but his legacy as one of Bulgaria's greatest wrestlers lives on.
  • In his honor, the Dan Kolov-Nikola Petrov International Wrestling Tournament was established in 1973 and has since become one of the most prestigious wrestling events in the world.
  • Kolov was posthumously inducted into the United World Wrestling Hall of Fame in 2014 for his contributions to the sport of wrestling.
